Equipment Checkout App

Detailed Planning Questionnaire

To our Broadcast Engineering Team: The outlines and wireframes you provided are a fantastic start! This updated questionnaire digs into the specific details based on your ideas. Please be as thorough as possible.

Section 1: Reservation Rules & Policies

Your outline has set blocks (e.g., 9am-2pm). If a student needs a mic for just one hour, do they still have to reserve the entire 5-hour block?

How does a student "extend" a reservation? Do they request it? Does staff need to approve it? What happens if someone else has it booked for the next block?

Can any student check out any piece of gear, or are some items (e.g., advanced cameras) restricted to students in specific classes?

Besides the "3 blocks per week" rule, can a student check out multiple *separate* items at once (e.g., 3 different cameras for one project)?

Section 3: Staff Experience (Admin View)

Does a staff member need to approve a student's reservation *before* it's confirmed, or can students reserve available gear instantly?

The QR code scanner for check-in/out is a great idea. What device will staff use to scan? A computer with a scanner, a tablet, or their own mobile phones?

Will staff ever need to override the rules? For example, checking out gear to a professor who doesn't follow the 3-block limit, or booking an item far in the future?

Section 4: The Equipment

Your outline lists great details to include. Is there anything else to track for an item? (e.g., Purchase Date, Last Maintenance, Firmware Version, etc.)

When a "Camera Kit" is reserved, does the app need to track the individual items inside it (e.g., Camera Body S/N 123, Lens S/N 456)? Or is tracking the kit as a single unit enough?

Section 5: Technical & Design Details

The outline mentions "University SSO or student ID & password." SSO (logging in with the standard university e-ID) is usually best. Do you see any reason why a separate login system would be needed?

After a student logs in, where does their information (Name, ID, Email) come from? Should the app pull it automatically from the university system, or do they need to type it in themselves the first time they use the app?
